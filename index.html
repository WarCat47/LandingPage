About

Hello! My name is Dhrubo Aditya. Born on January of 2000 and raised in Bangladesh, my journey has been fueled by curiosity and a passion for technology. After starting my O'Levels classes, I moved to New York in 2016 and attended William Cullen Bryant High School. My high school years were marked by an intense focus on tech-related classes, including robotics, computer science, and AP Computer Science in which I achieved a perfect score.

Currently, I am pursuing a major in Computer Engineering at City Tech, continuing my academic journey in the field I love. Beyond my studies, I enjoy gaming, building PCs, and making sketches! Click Here to check them out.

I also love outdoor activities such as exploring, hiking, going to the gym, and cycling. I am a curious individual, always eager to understand how things work. My interests have led me to conduct extensive research on various topics, especially space and technology. I enjoy diving deep into theories and expanding my knowledge base.Thank you for visiting my site and also check my work life!.

Work

My journey into the workforce began almost immediately after I moved to New York at the age of 16. Over the years, I have held various positions that have honed my skills and shaped my career.

One of my significant roles was as a Parts Sales Manager at AutoZone. In this position, I focused on inventory management and customer satisfaction. I oversaw inventory operations to ensure optimal stock levels and presentation, assisted customers in identifying and locating the right parts, conducted diagnostics to recommend suitable products, and resolved customer issues. Additionally, I managed associate schedules and handled administrative tasks, implementing effective inventory restocking strategies and maintaining a visually appealing store layout to enhance the customer experience and drive sales. My role was pivotal in maintaining AutoZone's high standards of service, operational efficiency, and customer satisfaction. Currently, I work as a Store Manager at CubeSmart. In this role, I am responsible for all store functions, including debt collection, customer service, setting auctions, and maintaining overall store functionality. This position requires a high level of organizational skills and attention to detail to ensure the store runs smoothly and efficiently. My responsibilities include managing financial transactions, maintaining customer relationships, organizing and setting up auctions, and ensuring the store meets all operational standards.

Alongside my current job I am engaged in a fellowship with Headstarter AI, where I am gaining hands-on experience in various aspects of technology and artificial intelligence. My individual responsibilities include building a website and creating a pantry tracker, both of which enhance my technical skills and project management abilities. As part of a team of four, we are tasked with developing five different AI models. Our final team project aims to either obtain 1,000 users or generate $1,000 in revenue, challenging us to combine technical proficiency with strategic thinking and market understanding.

Each of these roles has contributed to my growth, teaching me the importance of hard work, adaptability, and customer service. These experiences have not only shaped my professional skills but also my approach to challenges and opportunities in the workplace.While my roles at AutoZone and CubeSmart may not directly relate to computer engineering, they have equipped me with invaluable skills such as problem-solving, customer service, and project management. These positions also highlight my adaptability and eagerness to learn, essential traits for any career. My ability to lead teams, resolve conflicts, and maintain operational efficiency will undoubtedly contribute to my success in future computer engineering roles. Additionally, my current fellowship with Headstarter AI is providing me with practical experience in web development, project management, and AI model building, further preparing me for a career in computer engineering.
